Your Nearest Community Makerspace

Looking for a makerspace near Plainwell, MI?

Kalamazoo Makerspace is located just minutes from Plainwell at 219 Peekstok Dr in south central Kalamazoor. We're a community-run, nonprofit workshop where members share access to professional tools, equipment, and workspace for woodworking, metalworking, 3D printing, laser cutting, electronics, and more.

What You Can Do at Our Makerspace

  • Woodworking projects - Build furniture, cabinets, home improvements, and custom pieces
  • Metal fabrication & welding - Work with steel, aluminum, and other metals
  • 3D printing - Prototype products, create custom parts, and bring designs to life
  • Laser cutting & engraving - Cut and engrave wood, acrylic, leather, and more
  • Electronics & robotics - Build circuits, program microcontrollers, and create tech projects
  • Mixed-media projects - Combine multiple tools and techniques for complex builds

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Kalamazoo Makerspace from Plainwell?

We're located approximately 5 minutes from Plainwell via M-43 (Gull Road) at 219 Peekstok Dr in south central Kalamazoor. The drive is quick and convenient from anywhere in the Plainwell area.

Do I need experience to join?

No experience necessary! We welcome makers of all skill levels, from complete beginners to experienced craftspeople. We provide safety orientations and tool training to help you get started.

What does membership include?

Membership includes 56 hours a week of access to the facility (after orientation), use of all tools and equipment (after training), storage space for projects, and access to our community of makers. Check our pricing page for specific membership tiers.

Can I just pay per visit instead of a membership?

We operate on a membership model rather than day passes. This helps build a strong community and keeps costs affordable for regular users. Memberships start at very reasonable monthly rates.

Do you provide materials for projects?

Members bring their own materials (wood, metal, filament, etc.). We provide the tools, equipment, workspace, and expertise. Some consumables like sandpaper and basic hardware may be available.

What are your hours?

Members have 56 hours a week of access after completing orientation. For tours and general inquiries, check our calendar or contact us to schedule a visit.
