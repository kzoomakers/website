What is a makerspace?

A makerspace is a shared workshop where people create, experiment, and build together—using tools that are rare, specialized, expensive, or simply too large for most individuals to own.

Most makerspaces operate in libraries, schools, or universities.

We are unique in being a membership funded non-profit organization.

How our makerspace works

  • We are a volunteer operated 501(c)(3) non-profit, nobody gets paid.
  • Everyone pitches in, whether it’s giving tours or taking out the trash
  • Our tool collection grows through donated and loaned gear from makers
  • Membership fees keep the lights on — rent, utilities, insurance, supplies, repairs, outreach
  • Safety first: members complete training before they’re turned loose on the machines.
