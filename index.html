What is a makerspace?

A makerspace is a shared, community-oriented workshop where people can design, prototype, and build things using tools, equipment, and digital fabrication technologies. Think of it as a hybrid between a workshop, a lab, and a creative classroom.

How this makerspace works

  • We are a 501(c)(3) non-profit, nobody gets paid and no profit is made.
  • Everyone volunteers where they can, from tours to emptying the trash.
  • Companies or individuals donate equipment and grow our tool collection.
  • Membership prices cover costs like rent, electric, gas, insurance, supplies, repairs, community outreach.
  • All members must complete safety and equipment training before independent use of machinery.
  • Members are responsible for maintaining cleanliness and properly storing tools after use.
